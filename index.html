Obesity in the United States (2018)


This visualization is designed to portray any correlations between obesity and multiple different factors of the United States population in 2018.




Navigate by either scrolling up and down or clicking the top-left buttons for each respective slide.

Obesity according to Income


This bar chart plots the average obesity of people of certain levels of income, in which people earning less than $15,000 have the highest average of obesity and people earning more than $75,000 have the lowest.


Hover your mouse over the bars to view exact percentages.

Obesity vs Income

Obesity according to Age


This bar chart plots the average obesity of people of certain ages, in which middle-aged people tend to have the highest average of obesity and the youngest tend to have the least.


Hover your mouse over the bars to view exact percentages.

Obesity vs Age

Obesity according to Education


This bar chart plots the average obesity of people of certain levels of education, in which people who have the lowest education have the highest average of obesity and people with the highest education have the lowest.


Hover your mouse over the bars to view exact percentages.

Obesity vs Education
